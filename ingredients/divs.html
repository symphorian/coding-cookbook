Div elements are the most basic block element in HTML.
As a reminder, block elements are usually used as containers for other elements. Block elements fill up all the horizontal space on the page (or inside their containers), and force a line break after the content inside them ends.
Div elements have no styling at all, and as a result, they can be considered the purest kind of container in HTML.
You can put inline elements inside of divs.

You can also put other block elements inside.

Or you can even include a mix of inline elements

and block elements.

The content inside a div will be grouped together in the code, but unless you use some kind of styling to represent that visually, you probably won't be able to tell on the page.
