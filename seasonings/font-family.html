Fallback Font Families

However, most of the time, we'd rather use a specific font family we like over the generic ones offered to us from the browser. But we have to remember that the font we want to use may not be installed on everyone's computer.

So rather than specifying a single font-family, which may or may not be available to use, we specify a comma-separated list of font families. That way, if the first family isn't available, we'll try the next one, and the next one, until we reach the final family, which should be one of the generic families.

For example, Helvetica, while popular, is generally only availabe on Macs. So to account for any Windows users, we add a fallback to a similar font called Arial. And if that one can't be found, then our last resort is the default sans-serif family.
