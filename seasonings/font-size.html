Additional Examples

The font-size property allows us to change the size of our text on the page. We can use both absolute units (like pixels) and relative units (like percentages and em's).

This text has a font-size of 60px.

This text has a font-size of 10px.

This text has a font-size of 33%, which is relative to its container. Since the div containing this paragraph has a font-size of 60px, that means that this paragraph has a computed font-size of roughly 20px (33% of 60px).

Meanwhile, this paragraph has a computed font-size of around 3px, since its container has a font-size of 10px, and 33% of 10px is about 3px.

Another unit we can use is the "em" unit, which is based off of the default size of the letter "M" in our font family in the container our text is in. 2em means the font-size is twice the default font-size.

This text has a font-size of 4.5em.
