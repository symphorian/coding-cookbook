Additional Examples

There are many different border properties, mostly focused on allowing you to change the width, style, and color of a border.

The border style property is the only one with special keywords, so we'll focus on a couple common border style values first, before combining all of the border properties together.

Combining Border Properties

This div has a border set on it, with the border's width, style, and color all set at once.

border: 10px solid coral;

It has a rather thick 10px border-width, a "solid" border-style, and a border-color of "coral".

However, as we'll see below, we can also target different parts of the border individually, and we can even target individual sides as well.

This div sets its border properties individually.

border-width: 5px;
border-style: dashed;
border-color: #8888FF;
margin: 24px 0px;
padding: 24px;

It has a thinner 5px border-width, a "dashed" border-style, and a border-color with a hexadecimal value of #8888FF.

We also gave it a little margin and padding. The margin gives it space OUTSIDE the border on the top and bottom, but not on the sides. The padding give it space equally on all sides INSIDE the border.

The extra space on the inside is beneficial for giving the inside content room to breathe, and the extra space on the outside is good for separating the div from the content around it.

This div looks a little wacky, just because it's showing how we can target individual sides at once, or even individual properties on a specific side.

border-top: 8px dotted green;
border-right: 8px dashed red;
border-bottom-color: blue;
border-bottom-style: solid;
border-bottom-width: 4px;

Just to be clear, borders can be added to any HTML element, not only div elements. But we tend to use borders more on elements used as containers, in order to group related content, and separate it from unrelated content.
