My WordPress site doesn't show up correctly for other people!


For some students, when you share (or submit) your WordPress site Pages or Posts, your most recent updates don't seem to show up for other people.

This is a relatively common issue in Pantheon, and it is caused by the web server hosting your site caching old version of the pages/posts you create. Luckily, there are two relatively simple ways to fix it!

Follow the instructions below if your instructor or other people can't see the beautiful updates you recently made to your site.

Option 1: Clearing the Site Caches in Pantheon

  1. First, log into Pantheon.io.

    After logging in, navigate to the Sites tab in the navigation sidebar, and open up the Site Dashboard for the site that is behaving badly.

  2. Now you should be on your Pantheon Site Dashboard (different from your WordPress Site Dashboard).

    Locate the button at the far right that says "Clear Caches".

    Click the button.

    After a couple of seconds, the web server cache on your site should be reset, and your site should now be viewable in all its glory!

Option 2: Clearing a Page Cache via URL

  1. Access the live version of the WordPress Page or Post that you want to fix.

    Go up and find the URL for the site webpage in the URL bar of your browser (ideally Chrome).

    We are going to modify it in the next step.

  2. Add the following text to the end of the URL

    ?nocache

    Then hit Enter/Return in the browser's URL bar to refresh the page, and force it to clear the cache, so you can see the updated page in all its glory!

    However, be aware that this method clears the cache for THIS PAGE ONLY.

Congrats! You should have been able to clear the caches for your site so you can share your updates with the world (and your instructor)!
