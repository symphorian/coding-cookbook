Installing a Google Fonts Plugin (WordPress)

Getting the free fonts you need onto your WordPress site

  1. One reminder before we get started: there's nothing wrong with using Plugins on your WordPress site; they're one of the features that makes WordPress even more customizable!

    BUT, not all plugins are well-written or well-maintained, and because of that, some may not do what you expect, or even worse, they could modify your site destructively (meaning that even after you deactivate/delete the Plugin, there are still side effects).

    So, if you do want to dabble in Plugins, we just urge you to be cautious, and make sure to check out the reviews and documentation before installing them, much like you should be careful about downloading anything off the internet.

    Now, with that out of the way, let's introduce you to a safe and useful Plugin we recommend!

  2. First, login to WordPress and navigate to your Dashboard.

    From there, click on the Plugins link in the left sidenav.

    You'll be taken to the Plugins view, from which you can see all currently installed Plugins on your site.

    There are a couple installed by default that you can ignore.

    Click on the [Add New Plugin] button at the top of the screen to continue.

  3. This will bring you to the Plugins "marketplace", where you can search for and install new Plugins to use on WordPress.

    It is structured very similar to how you would search for and install new Themes.

    Click into the "Search Plugins" text box at the top right, and type "Google Fonts".

    The view will automatically begin searching once you finish typing.

  4. After typing "Google Fonts", some search results should come up.

    The one we're looking for should be the first result that pops up in the top left (assuming you spelled "Google Fonts" correctly).

    The Plugin we want to use is called "Fonts Plugin | Use Google Fonts, Adobe Fonts or Upload Fonts". ...a bit wordy, right?

    Once you've found it, click the [Install Now] button.

  5. After the installation is successful, the button will change into an [Activate] button.

    Go ahead and click that next.

  6. After activating the Plugin, you'll be brought to a screen like this one, where they try to get your email address for a "Quickstart Guide".

    This page can be completely ignored; the Cookbook page you're on right now is your quickstart guide.

    Now that the Plugin is installed and activated, navigate to the Customize menu for your site.

  7. Back in your Customize Menu, you should see a new menu item called "Fonts Plugin", with the same little icon next to it associated with the Plugin.

    Go ahead and click on it.

  8. Now you'll find yourself looking at some new menu items inside of the Fonts Plugin.

    The items you'll most likely find yourself configuring will be the "Basic Settings" and maybe the "Advanced Settings", if necessary.

    Go ahead and click on "Basic Settings".

  9. Inside Basic Settings, you'll probably want to be setting the fonts for "Base Typography" and "Headings Typography".

    The former changes the default font family for the whole site, and the latter changes the font family used for headings (i.e. Heading Blocks) on the site.

    Go ahead and click on the dropdown under "Base Typography".

  10. After clicking on the dropdown, a search bar will appear, with a long list of fonts underneath it.

    You could scroll down through all of them to find what you're looking for, but it's probably a lot easier to search for the name.

    Once you find the font family you're looking for, just select it, and you should see the new font family applied to all of the basic non-heading text on the site!

    Go ahead and do the same for Heading Typography, assuming you need to, and all headings on the site will be updated to use that font family.

  11. If you're happy with those results, then you can stop right here.

    NOTE: Just make sure to click the [Publish] button at the top of the screen before you leave the Customize menu; otherwise, none of your changes will be saved!

    ...but, if you find that you want a bit more control over certain things on the site (for example, maybe you only want certain headings, like H1's and H2's, to have a certain font), then keep reading.

    Click on the back arrow [<] a couple of times to get back to where you can select the "Advanced Settings", and click on that.

  12. There are quite a few options in the Advanced Settings menu.

    You can explore the others if you like, but for the scenario we previously mentioned, we'll be heading into the "Content" section.

    Go ahead and click on "Content".

  13. There are EVEN MORE options as we go deeper in the Advanced Settings menu.

    As you can see, from here, we can configure the font families for individual headings. So if we want H1's and H2's to have a different font family compared to the other headings, this is where we can configure it.

    Simply pick the font from the appropriate dropdown menu, and you should be good to go.

    NOTE: again, just make sure to click the [Publish] button at the top of the screen before you leave the Customize menu; otherwise, none of your changes will be saved!

Congrats! You've learned how to use a Google Fonts Plugin!
