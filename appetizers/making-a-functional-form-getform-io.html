Making a Form Functional (getform.io)

So you want a form that does more than look good?

  1. This tutorial assumes you already have a completed form on your website.

    If you don't, check out this sample code from the Coding Cookbook!

    To make a form functional, we're going to use a service from a site called getform.io.

    Go to getform.io and click the green "Generate endpoint for free" button to start.

  2. Create an account with them by filling out the required information.

    NOTE: Don't use your school email address! Our school email server is a tad overprotective, and may not let the verification email through.

    Or, continue with your Google account.

  3. After successfully creating an account, getform.io will send an email to the email address you supplied when you created your account.

    Check your email inbox for the verification email.

  4. The verification email should look something like this.

    Click the Verify Email button in the email.

  5. After verifying your email, you'll be redirected back to getform.io, and the verification message should be gone.

    Click the [+ Create] button to create a new endpoint to use in your form.

  6. A "Create..." dialog will pop up.

    Make sure the "Form" tab is selected, and then provide a name for the endpoint.

    Something like "contact-form" is fine.

    Also select your timezone (although this field doesn't really matter).

    Then click the blue Create button.

  7. After the dialog closes, you will see the new endpoint you need, along with some instructions.

    Copy that form endpoint.

  8. Go to your HTML code, where your form.

    Add an attribute to the form called 'method', and give it the value "POST".

    Then add another attribute called 'action', and give it the form endpoint you copied from getform.io.

    Assuming your form is correctly built, that's all you need to get it working! Though you'll need to commit and push your changes for it to work for everyone else.

    Let's test it out by opening the page in the browser (using the "Open with Live Server" option).

    NOTE: The form will not work in the preview window inside Visual Studio Code; there are protections in there that will prevent you from visiting pages outside of your site.

  9. After opening the form in the browser, try filling it out and clicking the "Send" button.

  10. If everything went well, you should be redirected to a "Thank You!" screen.

  11. Now, if you jump back to getform.io, instead of the form endpoint, you should see the form data that you just submitted!

    Congrats! You can now access the submissions that people make on your Contact Form!

    What's more, you should also get an email whenever anyone submits a form. Very handy for responding quickly to potential clients!

Congrats! You've successfully created a functioning form!
