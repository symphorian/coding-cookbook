Creating a Post (WordPress)

Your First Step in Creating Timely CMS Content

  1. First off, If you haven't already, log in to your WordPress site, and navigate to the Dashboard.

    If you're on the live version of your site, but the Admin Bar doesn't show up at the top of the screen, you might have been logged out due to inactivity.

    Add /login to the end of the URL, and you'll be able to login again.

    Once on the Dashboard, click on the Posts link in the sidebar.

  2. The Dashboard will switch to the Posts view.

    Click the "Add New" button at the top.

    There are actually multiple ways to create a new Post in WordPress; have you noticed any of the other ways?

  3. WordPress will create a new empty Post for you, and navigate you to the Block Editor for that Post.

    Click on the placeholder text "Add title", and add the title that you want the Post to have.

  4. After adding in your title, all that's left is to actually add content to your post.

    To do so, you'll need to add some blocks to your Post.

    There are many different ways to add blocks to the Block Editor. You can:

    • click on the blue button with the + icon in the top left of the navbar.
    • click on the black button with the + icon to the far right of the current cursor location.
    • put the cursor in the placeholder text and follow the instructions to type a "/", which will bring up a dialog from which you can pick blocks from.

  5. When you're done and ready to publish your changes to the site, click on the blue "Publish" button in the top right of the navbar.

  6. Another sidebar will open up, asking you to confirm that you really want to publish.

    Click the blue "Publish" button again to confirm and publish your changes.

    You can also change some settings of the Post before you publish from this sidebar if you wish, such as the altering the the Visibility of the Post or its Publication Date (the default is "Immediately", but you can set it to publish for some time in the future).

Congrats! You've successfully created a WordPress Post and published it!
